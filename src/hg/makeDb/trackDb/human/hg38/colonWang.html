Description

This track shows data from Single-cell transcriptome analysis reveals differential nutrient absorption functions in human intestine. In this study, single-cell RNA sequencing was used to survey gene expression profiles of the epithelium in the human ileum, colon, and rectum and ultimately the differential expression of nutrient absorption related genes in these areas. There are two bar chart tracks in this track collection with colon cells grouped by either cell type and donor. The default track displayed is colon cells grouped by cell type.

Display Conventions

The cell types are colored by which class they belong to according to the following table. The coloring algorithm allows cells that show some blended characteristics to show blended colors so there will be some color variation within a class.

Method

Using 10x Genomics single-cell RNA sequencing, RNA profiles of intestinal epithelial cells were obtained for 4,472 cells from two human colon samples. The healthy intestine mucosa used was sampled away from the tumor site on surgically resected tissue belonging to two donors diagnosed with intestinal tumors. To remove mucus, blood cells, and muscle tissue the intestinal tissues were washed in cold HBSS buffer.
