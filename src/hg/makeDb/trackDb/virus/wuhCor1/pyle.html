Description

Genome-wide RNA Secondary Structure Measurements and Prediction

This track shows data from Anna Pyle's lab describing the secondary structure of the SARS-CoV-2 RNA genome. The authors perform in-silico analyses as well as experimental measurements using SHAPE (selective 2 hydroxyl acylation) to experimentally analyze the RNA structure of the SARS-CoV-2 genome.

This data is described in depth in de Cesaris Araujo Tavares et al for Shanon entropy values and Huston, Wan, et al for SHAPE data.

Display Conventions and Configuration

Two tracks are available:

Full Length Shannon Entropy: A genome-wide Shannon entropy profile derived from base pairing probabilities was computed using SuperFold. A low Shannon entropy (near 0) is evidence for RNA structure.

SHAPE reactivity: In-vivo SHAPE reactivity per nucleotide of SARS-CoV2 genome. A high positive score indicates that the nucleotide is accessible (not base-paired). A low score (close to 0) indicates the nucleotide is not accessible (base-paired). A score of -999 means no data for that nucleotide was recovered experimentally. For visualization purposes the minimum display value has been set to -1 and the max has been set to 10.

Note that the authors provide further data (including secondary structure predictions) at their github repository.

Methods

For Shannon entropy, comSuperFold was used with default settings to predict secondary structure for the full-length SARS-CoV-2 RNA genome.

For SHAPE_reactivity, VeroE6 cells were infected with 105 PFU of SARS133 CoV-2 isolate USA-WA1/2020. See the preprint for further details. wigToBigWig was used to convert wiggles from the author's github repository to bigWig files after filtering out headers.

The Github repository with all raw data files can be found here: https://github.com/pylelab/SARS-CoV-2_SHAPE_MaP_structure.
