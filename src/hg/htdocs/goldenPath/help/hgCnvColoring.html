Structural Variants Color Code

CNV items in the Genome Browser tracks are coloured by Variant Type. The categories can be:

  • Gain: duplications, triplications, tandem duplications, and copy number gains
  • Loss: deletions and copy number losses
  • Insertion: insertions, Indels, and mobile element insertions
  • Inversion: inversions
  • Structural Alteration: translocations, complex structural alterations and chromosomal rearrangements, fusions, and multi-allele CNVs
  • Other Sequence Alterations: NT expansions, tandem repeats, microsatellites, breakend, and undetermined or not defined alterations

Three levels of color intensity are used in CNV items to indicate the Clinical Significance. The lightest shade indicates benign/likely-benign variants, the darkest shade indicates pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants, and the medium shade indicates other or no clinical significance has been defined.
